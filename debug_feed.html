    Here to say something most brand managers already know… but can’t say out loud:



    Organic content isn’t budgeted because they don’t believe in it.  
    It’s because the people approving budgets don’t understand it.

    Not their fault. They’ve never built something from 0 → 100K.  
    They haven’t seen what consistent organic really does over time.

    So from their POV, it looks like a cost with no immediate return.
    And when you’re at the top, you optimise for what’s measurable quarterly - Paid ads, Campaigns, SEO…

    But, if your brand is growing solely on paid formats - I am sorry but you are just renting your clientele.

    Paid ads sure gives short-term revenue but organic content builds loyalty which brings people back again and again.

    The relevance of this might not seem big at the moment but it will show up later when performance starts getting expensive and nothing is pulling it back down.

    The brands that are getting this right don’t even behave like brands.

    Take TaxBuddy.com - we grew them from 2K to 100K+ through organic video content. Just by helping people understand how to save taxes. 0 paid push. Pure value.

    That's a brand people remember when tax season hits.

    If you're making marketing decisions at your company, know this:
    Someone in your space is already building that audience. Is it you?


    Mandar Kamath
    #OrganicMarketing #BrandBuilding #MarketingStrategy #GrowthMarketing #MarketingLeadership
